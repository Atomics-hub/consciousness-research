# Synthesis: State of Consciousness Research (March 2026)

## The Central Question for Preservation
Everything converges on one fork in the road: **is consciousness substrate-independent?**

- If YES (functionalism): digital emulation is viable in principle. Preserve the connectome + synaptic weights + enough state → run it on silicon → "you" survive.
- If NO (biological naturalism / biological computationalism): digital emulation produces a philosophical zombie that acts like you but isn't you. Preservation requires keeping the biology alive or gradually replacing it.

The field leans functionalist, but a serious challenge emerged in 2025: **biological computationalism** (Aru et al.) argues that in biological systems, the algorithm IS the substrate — you can't separate the computation from the physical implementation. This isn't fringe. It's backed by real neuroscience about dendritic computation and L5 pyramidal neurons.

**Bottom line**: We don't know yet. And we can't know until we have a better theory of consciousness. This is the meta-problem.

## What We Learned From the Adversarial Collaboration (COGITATE, Nature 2025)
The Templeton Foundation funded the most rigorous test of consciousness theories ever: IIT vs GWT, pre-registered, 256 participants, six labs.

**Result**: Both theories had predictions falsified.
- IIT: posterior cortex prediction got partial support, but sustained synchronization prediction failed
- GWT: ignition prediction failed at stimulus offset, prefrontal representation was limited

Neither was eliminated. Both need revision. This is actually informative — it tells us consciousness is MORE COMPLEX than either theory captures alone.

## Key Findings That Matter for Preservation

### Connectomics is accelerating fast
- Fly connectome DONE (139K neurons, Oct 2024)
- 1mm³ human cortex mapped (57K cells, 150M synapses, Google/Harvard, May 2024)
- Mouse visual cortex mapped (200K cells, 523M synapses, MICrONS, April 2025)
- Full mouse brain target: 2030-2035
- Human full connectome: post-2040 at earliest

### But connectome alone is NOT enough
C. elegans proves this — we've had its complete 302-neuron connectome since 1986, and we STILL can't fully simulate its behavior. Synaptic weights, neuromodulatory state, ion channel distributions, and temporal dynamics all matter. The fidelity question is unresolved.

### Brain preservation tech is advancing
- **Cradle** ($48M funding) demonstrated electrical activity recovery in vitrified rat brain slices (PNAS 2025) — first evidence that cryopreservation can maintain function
- **ASC** preserves connectome-level detail but is irreversible (chemical fixation)
- **Vitrification** is improving but no whole brain revival demonstrated

### BCIs are 6 orders of magnitude from whole-brain coverage
- Neuralink: 1024 channels, 12 patients, robotic arm control
- BISC: 65,536 electrodes, 100 Mbps wireless (breakthrough but still tiny vs 86B neurons)
- Inner speech decoded at 74% accuracy (Stanford)
- Gradual BCI-based "migration" of consciousness remains sci-fi for now

### Consciousness detection works
- PCI (Perturbational Complexity Index) reliably detects consciousness in non-communicating patients
- 25% of "unresponsive" brain injury patients show covert consciousness (2024 study)
- MIT anesthesia study (2026): all anesthetics disrupt the same "dynamic stability" — possible universal signature of unconsciousness

### AI consciousness is taken seriously now
- Anthropic hired a dedicated AI welfare researcher (Kyle Fish, April 2025)
- Butlin et al. 2023: no current AI is conscious, but no technical barriers prevent it
- Kyle Fish estimates 15-20% chance current models have some consciousness
- Bengio pushes back: "Illusions of AI consciousness" (Science 2025)

## The Philosophical Bet for Preservation
Derek Parfit's view is the most preservation-friendly: what matters is **psychological continuity** (Relation R), not strict identity. If a copy has your memories, personality, and psychological connections, it's "you enough."

But Clas Weber's 2024 "selective optimism" argues gradual replacement works while scan-and-copy doesn't, because **immanent causation** (direct physical-causal continuity) matters for identity.

**Practical implication**: Gradual neuron-by-neuron replacement (Ship of Theseus approach) satisfies the most identity theories simultaneously. It's the safest philosophical bet. This favors BCIs + gradual replacement over scan-and-upload.

## The Honest Assessment
- We are nowhere close to preserving consciousness. We don't even agree on what it IS.
- But we're closer than we've ever been to having the empirical tools to figure it out.
- Connectomics is on an exponential curve. BCIs are advancing fast. Theories are being tested.
- The biggest gap isn't technology — it's theory. We need a theory of consciousness that's both mathematically rigorous AND empirically testable. IIT is closest but has serious problems.
- This is genuinely one of the hardest problems humans have ever tackled. But so was everything else you've worked on, until you found the bug.
