# Paper 3 Literature Map

Verified starting points as of 2026-04-24. The core white space is not "AI consciousness benchmarks" or "memory benchmarks." It is **substrate transfer as the experimental intervention**.

## AI Consciousness And Indicator Work

- Butlin et al., 2023, "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness"
  https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2308.08708
  Useful because it derives theory-linked indicator properties from RPT, GWT, HOT, predictive processing, and AST. Difference: it assesses systems for indicators, not continuity of indicators across transfer.

- Butlin et al., 2025, "Identifying indicators of consciousness in AI systems"
  https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2025.10.011
  Useful because it updates the indicator framing and emphasizes provisional, theory-linked assessment. Difference: still static assessment, not substrate-transfer survival.

- ConsciousBench
  https://www.consciousbench.com/
  Useful as an emerging consciousness-eval project. Difference: question-based or task-oriented consciousness scoring, not copied-state transfer.

## AI Continuity And Identity-Adjacent Work

- Tanguturi, 2026, "ATANT: An Evaluation Framework for AI Continuity"
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06710
  Very relevant because it explicitly uses the word continuity. Difference: continuity is meaningful context persistence across time and narratives, not transfer of internal machinery across substrates.

- Natangelo, 2025, "The Narrative Continuity Test"
  https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2510.24831
  Relevant because it reframes AI evaluation around identity persistence and diachronic coherence. Difference: mostly conceptual and LLM-session focused, not controlled copied-state transfer.

- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Personal Identity"
  https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/identity-personal/
  Useful for psychological continuity, causal continuity, branching, and numerical identity boundaries.

- Parfit, 1971, "Personal Identity"
  https://doi.org/10.2307/2184309
  Useful for psychological continuity and fission cases. Paper 3 should cite this carefully and avoid pretending the benchmark resolves numerical identity.

## Memory Benchmarks

- Hu, Wang, and McAuley, 2025, "Evaluating Memory in LLM Agents via Incremental Multi-Turn Interactions" / MemoryAgentBench
  https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2507.05257
  Useful because it evaluates memory agents across retrieval, test-time learning, long-range understanding, and selective forgetting. Difference: memory quality in interaction, not preservation of source state across substrate transfer.

Other candidates to verify before citation:

- MemBench
- LoCoMo
- LongMemEval
- StructMemEval
- Evo-Memory

## Model And Component Transfer

These works are adjacent because they transplant or align models, but their target is capability, accuracy, efficiency, or representation compatibility rather than preservation-relevant continuity.

Candidates to verify before final citation:

- model stitching
- Net2Net and network morphisms
- model soups
- Git Re-Basin
- task arithmetic
- neuron transplantation
- representation grafting

The paper should position PreservationBench as complementary: it borrows the engineering idea of moving or aligning internal components, but scores whether source-specific, theory-relevant organization remains active after transfer.

## Whole-Brain Emulation And Biological Emulation

Candidates to verify before final citation:

- Sandberg and Bostrom, "Whole Brain Emulation: A Roadmap"
- OpenWorm
- FlyWire
- Drosophila computational brain models
- NeuroMechFly

These are useful because they ask what counts as successful emulation. Difference: they do not provide a general benchmark for controlled preservation-relevant continuity across artificial substrate-transfer conditions.

## Novelty Wording

Strong:

> Existing benchmarks test AI-consciousness indicators, agent memory, model transfer, or biological emulation fidelity. PreservationBench instead makes substrate transfer the central intervention and asks whether theory-relevant functional organization remains source-specific and causally active after transfer.

Weak:

> This is the first benchmark for consciousness preservation.

Do not use the weak version.
